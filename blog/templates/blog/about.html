history of taccf

➢the beginning

‘’Write the vision, and make it plain upon tables, that he may run that readeth it. For the vision is yet for an appointed time,’’ says the Word of the Lord in Habakkuk 2:2-3. For every new beginning, there requires a recording process, for posterity sake and for memories of a landmark achievement.

All over Nigeria, The Apostolic Church Nigeria commonly abbreviated as TACN is one of the renowned Pentecostal churches with its spheres of influence in every known part and tribe of the country. Some of the church's territories are LAWNA, Maritime, Akwa Ibom, Cross Rivers, Igboland etc. It is not limited to Nigeria, but known all over the world. The National headquarters is in Lagos. TACN has a tertiary student arm known as The Apostolic Church Students’ Fellowship of Nigeria (TACSFON).












➢The Uncommon Beginning

As earlier stated, TACN has a student arm called TACSFON, but rarely was it heard or seen that it has an arm for Youth Corps Members, hence Maritime Territory became one of the territories recorded in the history of TACN to birth a Corpers' Fellowship. However, this was not without its challenges. The TACCF story goes thus:
TACCF stands for The Apostolic Church Corpers' Fellowship.

TACCF in Rivers State started in 2017. This was precedented by the pioneer founder, Mr. Friday Ndiigbo when he went for his National Youth Service in 2010 in Sokoto State. He met TACCF Sokoto Chapter, which was quite established as it had already recorded 6 sets before his set. He met the Fellowship officials such as the executive members, the past executive members, commonly called the G-excos, patrons as well as matrons.

While there, he served as the Prayer Secretary, then became the President of the Fellowship. His experience in Sokoto State spurred him to make up his mind to see that TACCF is also established in Rivers State.

He began to take some steps to this effect on his return. He discussed with some Fathers in the Territory. Fathers like Pastor King Umossoh, Pastor Bassey Isaiah and Apostle (DR.) Timothy Adeniyi, the then outgoing President of TACSFON Rivers State Chapter. He, Mr. Friday Ndiigbo, was made the TACSFON NYSC coordinator in 2015. After this, moves were made to enter the NYSC Orientation camp in Nonwa Gbam in Tai Local Government Area of Rivers State. This was in a bid to get access to new members. However, due to the fact that churches were not allowed to operate in camp apart from the Nigeria Christian Corpers’ Fellowship (NCCF), the only recognized Christian fellowship in the camp, they could not go through. This almost made him give up as it seemed impossible at the time.

In 2016, it was announced that Maritime Territory will be hosting the TACSFON National Conference. During the preparation for the Conference, Mr. Friday had the opportunity to meet with Top NYSC officials as they had to make use of the NYSC orientation camp in Nonwa Gbam for the Conference. He seized the said opportunity to discuss some salient things as regarding the establishment of the Fellowship. Also, the then TACSFON National Coordinator Eld. Bola Ogunkoye was informed and he gave his consent.

October 2017 made the first opportunity to meet the 2017 Batch B in camp. Unknowingly to the pioneer team, the 2016-2017, The Apostolic Church Nonwa Area Council, headed by Pastor Charles Achonwa, had set up a committee to raise a Fellowship for the Corpers, being that the Area is where the orientation is sited, and coupled with the fact that they have witnessed other churches welcoming their members. But the initial challenge of not being able to gain access into the camp also limited the efforts of the Committee.

Nevertheless, the Committee was informed of the ongoing plans concerning the Fellowship. The Committee headed by Pastor Charles Achonwa contributed ideas and encouraged Mr. Friday Ndiigbo to start.

This he started as he had the privilege to enter camp and worship there. He joined other fellowships who visited every Sunday. He was able to meet some The Apostolic Church Members. On the last Sunday in camp when free access to camp was granted, he was able to meet with these members.

The success he could record was received with so much joy by the Fathers. Pastor Charles and Pastor Isaiah came to camp to meet with the newly found members during one of the Sunday services. This activated the first members of the Fellowship.

Unfortunately, during posting, the corps members were posted to different local government areas outside Port Harcourt. This made it impossible for them to be assembled in a place. Also, there was no lodge, commonly known as the family house in sight yet. This same challenge presented itself three batches after the first one.

All through this, Pastor Isaiah was a major supporter of the vision, financially, morally, physically, etc. He surrendered himself to the services of raising and seeing to it that the vision of establishing the Fellowship came through.

As stated earlier, the 2017 set were the first members of the fellowship, notably among them were Sister Shade Oni, Brother Segun, Brother Femi Akinola, Brother Kingsley Akpan, Sister Patience and Sister Grace. It was recorded by Mr. Friday that this set served the fellowship with their heart and soul. Several meetings were held as the running of the fellowship became burdensome due to financial constraints, non-availability of a fixed house for the fellowship, among other things. It was almost near giving up but fatherly words of encouragement from Fathers kept it going.

After all these, Evangelist now Pastor Nwachukwu was appointed the Maritime Territory Youth Coordinator and Mr. Friday Ndiigbo was appointed as an executive as the NYSC Coordinator under the territory. This called for a meeting and the first unofficial executive members were appointed. Brother Kingsley was the President and Brother Segun became the PRO. Sister Grace was the Sisters’ Coordinator and mother of the fellowship. This enabled administrative activities to be carried out. A WhatsApp page was created and letters containing the needs and intents of the fellowship were drafted and sent to the Territorial Chairman Apostle L. O. Ogbonna. Meetings were held with the Territorial Executive Members where they discussed the missions, visions and goals of the fellowship. This prompted the need to find a suitable family house.

TACCF VISION and mission

➢the vision

It is the vision of this fellowship to build leaders with proper understanding of the kingdom of God and ready to influence others through their gifting, intellect ministry, profession and career.












➢the mission

The NYSC programme runs through the year with at least three batches “A, B, C”, which sometimes has streams and the fellowship is saddled with the responsibility to establish relationship with incoming corps members within and after the camp. The fellowship embarks on Rural Rugged Evangelism, School Evangelism, Hospital Visitation, free-lectures, Community Development Service (CDS), Seminars, Leadership Training, Gospel Concert and other programmes that are targeted towards soul winning, giving hope to the hopless and kingdom expansion.

The Fellowship's Activities

So far, the fellowship has been able to embark on numerous activities so as to fulfil the vision and mission of the fellowship. God has blessed the fellowship with able men and women of spiritual soundness, these has equally been able to organise activities in the following areas:












➢ Sisters program

The first sisters' activity was a Sisters Weekend which took place on February 23rd 2019, it was tagged "A Praise Worthy Woman". This was anchored by the then first sisters' Coordinator of the fellowship who was also the Vice President/Bible Secretary- Sister Esther Abiola Aro. The program was of much importance to the development of the sisters wing. In attendance were sisters, brothers and speakers- Dcns. Deborah Adeniyi, Dcns Bassey Isaiah, Dcns Nwamadi was the chief host. The sisters weekend program also brought remarkable improvement to the fellowship' family house as a 12kg cooking gas cylinder was bought and its cooker among other things.












➢ TACCF Outreaches

TACCF in the spirit of fulfilling its divine mandate, has embark on various outreaches with the Evangelism/Follow Up Unit of the fellowship. The first official outreach made by the fellowship which was directed by the first Evangelism/ Follow-up secretaries- Bro. Timilehin Elufisan and Sis. Oluwatofunmi Aribisala (Asst) was the Hospital Evangelism conducted on February 9th, 2019 to Rivers State University Teaching Hospital in Port Hacourt. Also, street evangelism was embarked on to win more souls for Christ. Orphanage visitation was made to Lemu Orphanage on May 27, 2019 to show love to the less privileges. Follow up activities were able to ensure an all inclusive fellowship where members who were not posted to Port Harcourt could come around and fellowship. Their welfare is continually sought so as to ensure an all encompassing and functionally run fellowship.
Furthermore, the said unit is entrusted with the reception of new corps members from camp, with the grace of the Lord, the fellowship has been able to increase in number from every batch of NYSC members posted to Rivers State.












➢Other Activities include

The fellowship under the supervision of the first president of the fellowship Bro. Osalebenda Daniel Okolo organised the first seminar for the youth in the territory on April 4th 2019 at The Apostolic Church Rumuadara Assembly, Port Harcourt.
Visitation to churches with the supervision of the Excos and the General Coordinator- Mr. Friday Ndigbo. Churches are visited to worship with the members, to seek funds, to perform ministerial tasks such as drama, songs, talk show etc.
Visitation to members who are not in the in Port Harcourt too is usually done, sick members are not left out.












➢TACCF Patrons/Matrons/ Advisers

TACCF since its inception has been blessed with men and women of great value who in one way or the other have been a burden bearer for this fellowship. Life patrons Apostle Dr. Timothy Adeniyi and Pastor Isaiah Bassey together with several others that still play a huge role in the fellowship's growth have influenced the fellowship in the following ways; supports for a permanent family house, fellowship bus project, Evangelism activities, fellowship programs (sendforth, seminars), advisory, engagement, funding and many other more as the grace of the Lord permitted.
